Ben Katz

An intuitive parking kiosk for Tech Square at the Georgia Institute of Technology

Hive

Current parking kiosks are ridden with usability and interface issues, wasting time and money.

Current Issues

A disheartening amount of buttons and dispensers has no rhyme or reason, confusing users.

Kiosk's display is nearly impossible to read in bad lighting conditions.

Instructions for kiosk have too many steps, poorly organize information, and confuse users.

How can the interactions and experiences that users have with parking kiosks be enhanced?

Research

A task analysis was created to inform upon current kiosk usage flaws and possible improvement areas.

Visual observations, as well as both qualitative and quantitative data were collected to further an understanding of users and their struggles.

Design Objectives

With a solid base in user research to build upon, the following design objectives were developed.

The kiosk should be easily visible from a distance so users can locate the kiosk and quickly pay for parking.

The interface should only display necessary information step-by-step to reduce confusion and usage time.

The kiosk should mirror the Georgia Tech aesthetic scheme without standing out too much.

Form Concepting

After the general design objectives were defined, preliminary sketches were created to begin exploring possible forms.

More ideation

After an initial ideation phase was concluded, a form with a hexagonal base was chosen. Georgia Tech's aesthetic of yellow jackets and honeycombs informed this design choice.

Interface Ideation

Initial Construction

To get a sense of the kiosk's size and future usability, CAD and a rough full-scale prototype were constructed.

After construction, usability tests with paper wireframes were used to highlight areas of possible form and interface improvements. The physical form was to harsh and daunting. The first interface on the other hand, did not feel like it fit the aesthetic of the form. The yellow color selection and the wording of the call to actions were the main issues.

Refined Concepts

Several changes were made to both physical form to improve aesthetics and usability.

The physical form went through a few minor changes. The top of the kiosk was rounded off on both sides to match the cutouts on the sides. Additional cutouts were added on the bottom of the kiosk to reduce bulk.

Refined Interface

The interface went through several changes, including a progress bar, simplified visuals, and subtle usability improvements. The color scheme was changed to yellow to blue to match the usual color of parking areas while still fitting into the Georgia Tech environment.

Daytime and night screens were made to reduce glare and ease in readability and usability.
