Picture Perfect

Current Issues

Whether it's because of a bright flash or simply because someone isn't paying attention, blinking can often be a nuisance when trying to get the perfect snapshot.

Maybe something distracts you from smiling at the camera; perhaps you're still getting organized in a group photo. Either way, a great smile is a necessity of a great picture.

Trying to get everyone to pay attention, stand in the right place, and all smile together are only a part of what makes good groups photos hard to come by.

How can the experience of iPhone photography be more efficient and seamless?

Brainstorming

A rough mind map revealed general the following design opportunities- ease of use and minimal learning curve.

Prototyping

Low fidelity wireframes were sketched out to begin laying out core information.

High fidelity wireframes informed screen flow and user navigation.

Final App

Picture Perfect does all the hard work for you.

To take the guesswork out of photography, Picture Perfect uses facial recognition to make sure everyone is smiling and has their eyes open before a photo is taken.

An interface that is both intuitive and dynamic.

Inspired by the native iPhone camera app you're already familiar with, Picture Perfect's point-and-shoot interface feels second nature.

How It Works

User presses camera button to take photo

A box surround the user's head lets them know a picture is about to be taken

Picture is taken with user's eyes open and a great smile

Picture Perfect is available on the App Store for free today.
