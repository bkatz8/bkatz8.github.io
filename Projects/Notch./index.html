notch.

A bright, friendly desk organizer.

￼￼Let’s be honest, desks can be pretty boring, messy, chaotic, and uninspiring.

Make 10 Design Prompt

The only requirement for this design project was to create an product that solves a problem and make ten using rapid manufacturing techniques. My personal design objective was to design a product that creates a lively, fun atmosphere for creativity while effectively using space.

Inspiration

I noticed that I found the desk space in the studios at Georgia Tech incredibly boring and dull, so I decided to focus on makely the desk work space more exciting.

Make 10 Manufacturing: Round 1

  • Laser cutting the bases for notch out of 3/8 inch acrylic.

  • Thermoforming acrylic with a heat gun.

  • More thermoforming.

  • I used Copic markers on hand to make sure everything was lined up.

  • Finished form after bending.

  • First round of yellow paint and then a layer of painter's tape for the second color.

  • Notch in its final form for studio review.

  • Slots provide a perfect fit for markers, as well as space for sticky notes and extra pens.

  • Extra space to slide a small notbook into.

Make 10 Manufacturing: Round 2

I created a simple jig using long screws and a plank of wood to rapidly manufacture ten notch desk organizers. The ten models were then taped with painter's tape to cover one side and preserve the acrylic shine.

The final painting for the Make 10 event. For the Make 10 market, I decided to make all of the marker holders one color instead of two to save production time, cost, and to make sure the final product was as clean as possible.

Notch in CMYK

Notch comes in the cyan, magenta, yellow, and black color schemes, giving desk spaces a bright, bubbly personality.

Small Space with a Big Punch

Notch has plenty of space for ten Copic markers, eight skinnier markers, a set of sticky notes, scissors, and a small notebook. All this in a tiny footprint with plenty of room for keeping your workspace bright and bold.
