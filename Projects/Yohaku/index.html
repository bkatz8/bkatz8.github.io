余白

(yohaku)

A relaxing tool for pre-massage practices and guided meditation.

￼The technology surrounding the massage space usually focuses on the times during and after massage. But for the whole massage experience to be truly fufilling, the time period before is just as if not more important than any other stage.

Design Prompt + Team

The design criteria that this studio project was based on was to design and prototype an interactive product that improved the massage experience. I worked on this project with college Sean Mackey who's portfolio you should check out!

Initial Ideation

I completed a round of concept ideation to identify which phase of the massage process would be best to target for the remainder of the project.

To get some real world experience in the massage world, Sean and I visited Sister Carolyn Ann Salzman at her practice in Decatur to gain insight into possible design solutions after an initial round of ideation. After visiting sister Carolyn, we decided to pursue meditation as an avenue of pre-massage relaxation.

Inspiration + Form Concepting

After looking for existing forms that lend themselves to relaxation and the human body, we selected the incense burner as a starting point.

Meet yohaku.

余白

def: White space, left deliberately with the intention to allow people to have a space to go.

So what exact does yohaku have to do with meditation? Essentially, yohaku means to find white space within the mind while meditating in order to find relaxation.

Yohaku Features

Yohaku excourages proper meditation poster.

Yohaku's integrated light bar helps guide users through breathing and mindfulness exercises.

Design Materials

The maple wood top and aluminum bottom are cool to the touch and help center users’ energy.

Speakers guide users through meditation while their eyes are closed.

Secondary Therapies

Aroma therapy aids in meditation by further augmenting the massage experience and relaxing users with various scents.
