FullStory

A summer of designing for a new way to look at customer experience.

Magic Moments

One of my first projects was working on the UX/UI for FullStory's "Magic Moments." What are magic moments you ask? They're moments of 'magical' insight that FullStory pulls from a user session to better help you understand how customers are using you're website!

Magic Moments Blog Post!

Making Some Magic

Understanding clicks, scrolling, and page transitions is great for understanding the basics. I realized that UX services should bring emotional experiences (especially emotional pain points) to light.

Conveying Physical Emotion in a Digital Space

Emotion is expressed with “facial expression, behavioral response, and physical responses, which include physiological, postural, and vocal changes.”

- Charles Darwin

Iteration

I went through several iterations on how to convey emotional painpoints through facial expressions. I relaized this- emotion is not an on/off switch. So a gradient of emotion over time was key to effectively displaying (in this case) growing “rage” over latency issues.

More Iteration...

I also came to the conclusion while exploring different sttes for magic moments that changes to both facial expression and color over time were superfluous.

Final Proposed Solution

Solves major design challenge by providing users an interaction and UI to identify UX pain points.

Magic Moments are a user favorite and spurred competitor Mouseflow to create a “click rage” feature.
