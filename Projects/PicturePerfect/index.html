Design Inspiration

One summer myself and a good friend Andrew Amontree (whose website you can check out here!) were fed up with taking pictures at social events and never getting the right shot. Our combined efforts in computer science and design allowed us to create a proof of concept that solved the issues presented in iPhone photography.

Current Issues with iPhone Photography

People bink at inconvinient times

Whether it's because of a bright flash or simply because someone isn't paying attention, blinking can often be a nuisance when trying to get the perfect snapshot.

Sometimes we aren't smiling at the right moment

Maybe something distracts you from smiling at the camera; perhaps you're still getting organized in a group photo. Either way, a great smile is a necessity of a great picture.

More people equals more problems

Trying to get everyone to pay attention, stand in the right place, and all smile together are only a part of what makes good groups photos hard to come by.

Knowing these issues, how can the experience of iPhone photography be redesigned to be more efficient and seamless?

Brainstorming

Prototyping
